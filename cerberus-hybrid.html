Report of the Vice-President,
University & External Affairs

It’s been a busy 2 months. I organized a September meeting at UTFA of the University of Toronto Employee Associations and Unions. Groups attending included USW Local 1998; CUPE Locals 1230, 3902, 3907, and 2484, OPSEU, IBEW, IUPAT, UNITE HERE, and APUS. Contracting out, upcoming bargaining and pensions dominated the agenda. Kudos to Terezia Zoric, VP Grievances, and the Grievance Committee (which I join), on the UTFA response to the proposed University Policy on Sexual Harassment. I am working with our President and the Membership Committee toward looking outward to international issues and to that end toward bringing in speakers at events for our members. We invite suggestions for a C.B. Macpherson lecturer on international or other topics.

— Linda Kohn
Report of the Treasurer

The financial status of the association is exceptionally strong. The audited financial statements show a surplus of $449,382 for the year ended June 30, 2016, despite an investment loss of $17,637. The average surplus over the last 10 years has been in excess of $230,000, bringing the association's assets to grow from about $2,000,000 to over $4,300,000 during the same period. To put these numbers in perspective, average dues per non-retired member are close to $1,000, but only about $850 were needed on average over the last several years. The year ahead looks at least as strong. Excluding investment returns, the 2016-17 budget approved by Council forecasts a $358,000 surplus, and investment returns are in excess of $95,000 in the first quarter of the year. The longer term perspective also looks positive as the steady growth in membership – hence dues income – experienced over the last 10 years is expected to continue.

— Ettore Damiano

Report of the Chair of the
Equity Committee
My name is Roy Gillis, an associate professor in the Department of Applied Psychology and Human Development, and the new Chair of the Equity Committee for the University of Toronto Faculty Association (UTFA). I have had a very longtime interest in equity and diversity issues in my research, teaching, and activism, illustrated by my efforts in such topics as sexual orientation and gender identity, violence against women, HIV prevention, bullying in schools and the psychological impacts of hate crimes, prejudice and discrimination. I have been an UTFA Council representative for the Ontario Institute for Studies in Education constituency since 2005, and was elected to the UTFA executive this past May. I am excited to bring my skills and enthusiasm to the Equity Committee, and hope to meet many of you through the various forums UTFA and the Equity Committee events we are planning. In fact, I think the term “diversity, equity, and inclusiveness” is a better descriptor of the vision I have for the Equity Committee of UTFA. This past summer has been a busy one for the Equity portfolio, and I have been involved in the consultation process, along with other UTFA executive members, for the new sexual violence policy at the University of Toronto, long term disability consultations, and the UTFA statement on protecting the rights of “trans” members of our University of Toronto community. I look forward to sharing with you more of the new and exciting initiatives that your Equity Committee is working on for all UTFA members in our next newsletter.

Report of the Chair of the
Librarians Committee

The Librarians Committee had its first meeting, which included eager new members, seasoned previous members, and representation from the three campuses. Ideas were bubbling over and all were excited to get the outreach and community connection ball rolling. In forthcoming communications, we look forward to relaying the years of community input that was distilled and used as the guiding principles for the continuing work of the ACALP group. We are interested in how the community rates the importance of these principles; thus, later this semester we will be circulating a short survey to garner your thoughts. Please visit the Librarians Committee page on the UTFA website to see the list of those serving on this committee, all of whom are able to answer any questions or refer you to someone who can. Additionally, you are welcome to submit your comments, questions, or suggestions regarding the current Policies for Librarians via the anonymous commentary/query page. The information submitted through the form will help us integrate your thoughts. We are eager to follow up with those who would like a response to their feedback. If you provide your name and email address in the boxes below, we will respond to you. If you would like to remain anonymous this form supports that option too. Looking forward to a collegial, inclusive and transparent year.

— Kathleen Scheaffer
